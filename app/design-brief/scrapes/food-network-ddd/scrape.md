# UX Analysis: Food Network DDD
URL: https://www.foodnetwork.com/shows/diners-drive-ins-and-dives

## UX Patterns Extracted
- **Official DDD color palette:** Bright reds and oranges (e.g., `#DD3333` or similar fire/red tones) prominently used in the official show logo. The surrounding site uses a clean white background with dark text to let the show's vibrant colors pop.
- **Typography choices:** Clean, highly legible sans-serif font standard across Food Network properties. 
- **Episode/video card layout:** Structured neatly with Season/Episode badges (e.g., "S54 | E1"), a bold episode title, a short 1-sentence description, and a clear "View Recipes" or "Watch Video" call-to-action button underneath. The layout is usually accompanied by a thumbnail image.
- **How restaurant info is presented within show context:** Rather than a raw database/map of locations, the official site presents curated collections or galleries such as "10 Can't-Miss Drive-Ins" or "The Best DDD in Nashville". They are treated as editorial content or slideshows.
- **Header/nav treatment:** Heavy corporate navigation featuring all Food Network shows, chefs, recipes, and videos. The show-specific navigation is minimal, mostly dropping you into "The Latest" articles or the "Episodes" tab.
- **Guy Fieri imagery style:** Action-oriented and vibrant. Guy is often shown driving his classic red Camaro, eating, or posing energetically, reinforcing his high-energy brand.
